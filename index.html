// 02 — The Project

Sahara Ventus

Egypt's progress is impeded by several grand challenges. This project addresses pollution and improving the use of alternative energies. The problem to be solved is reducing pollution by using alternative energies in Kharga Oasis, New Valley, where the local population depends entirely on diesel generators for electricity, which emit carbon dioxide, nitrogen oxides, sulfur oxides, and particulate matter.

Nitrogen oxides contribute to smog formation and respiratory disease; sulfur oxides produce acid rain that degrades soil and biodiversity; particulate matter penetrates lung tissue, causing cardiovascular illness. Therefore, the purpose of the study is to design a renewable energy system to replace diesel generation as the community's primary power source.

A Savonius VAWT wind turbine was designed using locally available resources — aluminum and MDF wood — targeting a power output sufficient to exceed baseline energy demands. The prototype must generate a minimum of 150 Joules under average local wind conditions, initiate movement at 4 m/s, withstand 60 °C without deformation, while not exceeding 0.25 m³ in volume.

The prototype underwent performance testing, and results demonstrated that the system successfully exceeds the required power output and operated reliably, offering a viable, clean alternative that directly reduces pollution and improves community health outcomes in El Kharga Oasis.

Egypt's recent evolution and development has many goals, two of which are to improve the use of alternative energies and address and reduce pollution fouling our air, water, and soil. The lack of use of alternative energies is mainly tied to the dependency on fossil fuels, as Egypt uses fossil fuels to generate over 94% of its energy. The heavy reliance on fossil fuels directly arises severe pollution due to the emissions it produces during combustion. Fossil fuel consumption produces many pollutants, such as carbon dioxide, which is emitted in large amounts annually, roughly reaching 242 Megatonnes in 2023.

The problem to be solved is the heavy reliance on diesel generators to generate electricity in El Kharga oasis. In El Kharga oasis, the only energy source is diesel generators, which are used to produce 100% of the energy there. El Kharga oasis depends on diesel generators to achieve its energy demands, which are estimated to be 200 kWh/day. Diesel generators are the main reason for the elevation of PAH concentrations in El Kharga's sediments, reaching up to 790.14 ng/g of dry weight — considered harmful to the environment and a significant health risk.

Two prior solutions were considered. The first is the Zafarana wind farm, which is reliable but significantly expensive — each HAWT unit costs 20 million dollars to manufacture and install. The second is small-scale vertical-axis wind turbines in Mang, Pakistan, which achieve environmental sustainability through wind energy but suffer from reduced efficiency.

The chosen solution is a Savonius vertical-axis wind turbine — a drag-based wind turbine consisting of two curved blades with an overlap of 5% for increased pressure difference and higher efficiency. Guide vanes were added to accelerate and redirect wind toward the blades, boosting performance significantly.

All components were selected for local availability and structural performance requirements. The prototype was built using the following materials:

Material Quantity Usage
Aluminium Sheet 1 sheet (1×1 m, 0.5 mm thick) Turbine blades
Steel Pipe 1 m, 25 mm diameter Center shaft of prototype
Stud Rod 4 rods × 0.08 m each Attach blades to center shaft
Hex Nuts & Flat Washers 24 hex nuts, 16 washers Fix stud rods to blades and shaft
MDF Wood 3 sheets (1.22×1.22 m) Guide vanes construction
MDF Glue 2 packets Fix blades to tori and bearings
Ball Bearing 2 items Enable rotation, reduce friction
DC Generator 1 unit Convert mechanical to electrical energy

The prototype was found to be a huge success — able to generate more than 600 joules and 2 watts, exceeding the design requirements and resolving the problem using a Savonius wind turbine that mainly utilizes drag force. It demonstrates its capability of solving significant problems such as pollution and fostering the use of alternative energies.

Negative results were found and overcome. The project met the solution requirements of generating renewable, carbon-free electricity. The project had positive results, exceeding specified design requirements by generating more than 2 watts in 5 minutes in low-wind speeds with high efficiency, benefiting from prior solutions while avoiding their weaknesses and enhancing their positives.

The project is determined to be implemented in El Kharga Oasis, which has an electricity demand of 200 kWh/day — all currently produced by diesel generators. El Kharga Oasis has great potential for generating electricity from wind, with wind speeds ranging between 6.8 m/s and 7.9 m/s. Installing Savonius vertical-axis wind turbines will utilize this wind energy, reducing fossil fuel dependency, CO₂ emissions, and bringing economic growth to the region.

For the real-life application, two enhancements are recommended. First, a Maglev Dynamo — replacing the mechanical bearing with a magnetic bearing — reduces vibration by 30%, reduces friction to near zero, and achieves efficiency of approximately 96%. Second, replacing aluminum blades with glass fiber (GFRP) would reduce blade weight, lower rotational inertia, allow self-starting at lower wind speeds, and produce smoother aerodynamic profiles through complex 3D molding without joints or welds.

Leaving the problem unsolved will continue to affect human health, increase environmental damage, and cause economic instability in the region.
